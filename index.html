Tiered Mentorship

This program is unique in that we have the students mentor each other. Faculty mentor Medical Students, Medical Students mentor Undergratuates, and Undergraduates mentor High School Students.

The program runs year long and we meet weekly from 3:30-5 during the High School year. Summer hours vary but usually last longer than 2 hours.

Getting to meet with doctors and nurses to ask questions about their career and even participate in hands on learning events can make all the difference in motivation.

Meeting VIPs, improving business skills, and gaining insight into eductation and careers are just some of the many wonderful benefits to having a mentor.

a nurturing atmosphere for learning.

Where/When

We meet weekly at the University of Kentucky's Gill Heart Institute located at 900 S Limestone, just left of the Albert B. Chandler Center. During the High School year (August - June) we meet once a week from 3:30 - 5pm. During the summer the hours vary but are generally longer and also up to the schedule of the students.

What

Students work with appropriate mentors according to their interests from Neuroscience to OBGYN. There are however common activites such as learning Library Sciences and how to conduct ethical research. The first year of Junior Resaerchers is composed of many of these common activites to help everyone stay certified to be in the hospital and to learn skills that will help them to stand out in the future.

Who

The director and Program coordinators select exemplery faculty to mentor students. Undergraduate and Medical School mentors are selected based on merit and experience with similar programs. All of our mentors have had stellar academic careers and carry a positive attitude about mentoring!

Why

We believe that in order to increase STEM interest from diverse students we must engage them at a young age. We believe that by targeting young students we can focus their interest on what they really like and show them that their goals are possible to attian.
